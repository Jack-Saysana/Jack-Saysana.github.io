Brainstorming

  • Develop a completely unstructured list of potential ideas, qualities, solutions and approaches
  • Ideas need not be complete or fully proven

Prototyping

  • Develop low-fidelity protoypes of brainstormed ideas which seem promising/attractive
  • Prototype has less focus on the visual, more focus on the functional
  • Prototypes are typically rough, digital mock-ups

Feedback

  • Perform critical self-reflective analysis and completed prototype to evaluate strengths and weaknesses
  • Utilize user feedback from desired audience to further parse potential changes

Implementation

  • With a promising prototype and relevant feedback, begin creating the final product
  • More attention granted to visual detailing
  • Refine techincal/functional details

Iteration

  • A single "run-through" of this process will not result in a quality result
  • Constantly refine via iteration and testing
  • Fall back to brainstorming and prototyping phases if later phases fail
