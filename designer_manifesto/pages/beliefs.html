Efficiency

  • Interfaces must prioritize the technical needs of users
  • Menu traversals, button clicks and mouse movement should be minimized
  • Digital pages must be lightweight and have minimal scripting as to ensure fast load times and minimal hitches

Learning Curve

  • Appealing to the lowest common user of an interface lowers the ceiling of potential for many important interfaces
  • Common interfaces should provide tools to superusers for high efficiency traversal and usage
  • Interfaces should be designed with their specific users and workflows in mind, without over generalization

Aesthetics

  • Minimalist and "flat" design languages are a tired trend in the context of modern digital interfaces
  • Novel and visually distinctive user interfaces increase eagerness to learn and adapt to new software
